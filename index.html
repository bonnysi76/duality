Wretched Man That I Am

“Wretched man that I am! who shall deliver me from the body of this death?”
—Romans 7:24
Lo, I am compassed with the weight of my vile estate,
For that which I do I allow not: what I would, that do I not;
But what I hate, that do I (Romans 7:15).
My flesh hath chained me to corruption,
And I find another law in my members,
Warring against the law of my mind (Romans 7:23).
I was drawn away of mine own lust, and enticed (James 1:14),
Lust conceived, and it brought forth sin,
And sin, when it was finished, brought forth death (James 1:15).
Mine eyes have dealt treacherously with me,
Though I made a covenant with them in youth (Job 31:1),
Yet they lead me as an ox to the slaughter (Proverbs 7:22).
She hath cast down many wounded: yea, many strong men
Have been slain by her (Proverbs 7:26).
O that one would give me waters for my head,
And a fountain of tears for mine eyes,
For I am wounded within (Jeremiah 9:1).
The plague is in mine own heart (1 Kings 8:38),
And there is no healing in my bones because of my sin (Psalm 38:3).
Though I fasted, He regarded it not (Isaiah 58:3),
And though I cried, He shut out my prayer (Lamentations 3:8).
My sins have hid His face from me,
That He will not hear (Isaiah 59:2).
I washed with nitre, and used much soap,
Yet mine iniquity is marked before Him (Jeremiah 2:22).
Woe is me, for I am undone (Isaiah 6:5),
Mine iniquities have taken hold upon me,
So that I am not able to look up (Psalm 40:12).
My soul cleaveth unto the dust (Psalm 119:25),
My heart is wax; it is melted in the midst of my bowels (Psalm 22:14).
I am weary with my groaning;
All the night make I my bed to swim;
I water my couch with my tears (Psalm 6:6).
The sorrows of death compassed me,
And the pains of hell gat hold upon me:
I found trouble and sorrow (Psalm 116:3).
Deliver me out of the mire,
And let me not sink (Psalm 69:14);
Bring my soul out of prison,
That I may praise Thy name (Psalm 142:7).
O God, create in me a clean heart (Psalm 51:10),
And renew a right spirit within me.
